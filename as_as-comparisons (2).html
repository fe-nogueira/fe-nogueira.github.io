Grammar Focus ~10 min
As...As and Less: The Rules

Two ways to compare — equal and not equal

As + adjective/adverb + As
as + adjective + as
= they are equal
Less + adjective/adverb + Than
less + adjective + than
= not equal (lower)
Read the example. Tap the correct meaning:

"Amazon Prime is as expensive as Netflix."

"Netflix is less expensive than HBO."

Important! Not as...as = Less...than (same meaning!)

Netflix is not as easy to use as HBO. = Netflix is less easy to use than HBO.
Amazon Prime doesn't update as often as HBO. = Amazon Prime updates less often than HBO.
Check your understanding — say it out loud!
Look at the chart from before. Make 2 sentences using "as...as" and 2 using "less...than" about Netflix, Amazon Prime, and HBO.
Example: "HBO's membership fee is as cheap as Amazon Prime's." / "Netflix is less easy to use than HBO."

Grammar Transformation ~8 min
Rewrite the sentences

Rewrite each sentence using the word in brackets. The meaning stays the same!

Remember the pattern:
less + adj + than ↔ not as + adj + as
"My watch is less attractive than yours." = "My watch isn't as attractive as yours."
1. Sally's shoes aren't as fancy as Jill's. (less)
2. Adam exercises less frequently than he used to. (not as...as)
3. This supermarket isn't as expensive as the one across the street. (less)
4. This gym is less convenient than the one near my house. (not as...as)
Pair check
Read your answers to your teacher. Explain WHY you used "less" or "not as...as". What's the difference in formality?
Tip: "not as...as" is more common in speaking. "less...than" is a bit more formal.

Wrap-up ~4 min
Review & Homework

Great work today! Let's review what you learned.

Today's grammar — quick summary
• Equal: as + adj/adv + as → "HBO is as cheap as Amazon Prime."
• Not equal: less + adj/adv + than → "Netflix is less easy to use than HBO."
• Not equal: not as + adj/adv + as → "Netflix isn't as easy to use as HBO."
• Remember: "less...than" and "not as...as" have the SAME meaning!
