Lesson objectives
By the end of the lesson, the student will be able to:
Name 10+ rooms and spaces in a home and describe what people do in each
Use there is / there are and basic question words (how many, where, what) to describe their own home
Understand a short video about a real New York apartment and answer comprehension questions
Express preferences and give simple reasons using I think… because…
Phase 1 — Warm-up
0:00

Warm-up · Discussion
What do you like about your home?
Open the lesson with the three discussion questions from slide 3. Keep this conversational — the goal is to activate background knowledge and get the student talking from minute one.
  • What do you like about your home?
  • What don't you like about it?
  • Do you prefer to live in a flat or a house? Why?
Teacher note: Don't correct heavily here — just listen and note vocabulary gaps for later. If the student is very quiet, prompt with "Tell me one thing in your home you love."

Phase 4 — Speaking (opinion)
0:37
8 min
Speaking · Opinion
Do you agree? (slide 20)
Use the four opinion statements from slide 20. Introduce the useful language frame first: I think / I don't think… because… / I agree / I don't agree because…
  • The flat in the video is too small for the family.
  • If you live in a small flat, you don't have to clean it very often.
  • It's better to live in a small flat in New York than in a big flat in a small city.
  • It's better to live in a small flat you own than in a big flat you don't own.
Teacher note: Model the first statement yourself with a strong opinion to show the student it's safe to disagree. Aim for 2–3 sentences per statement — don't let single-word answers pass without a "because…?"

Phase 6 — Production & wrap-up
0:57

Production · Speaking
Describe your dream home in 1 minute
Give the student 30 seconds to think, then ask them to describe their ideal home in 1 minute using the vocabulary from the lesson. Prompt with: "How many rooms? Is there a garden? Is there a gym?" Take notes on errors for the feedback moment below.
Teacher note: Don't interrupt. Let them finish, then give 2–3 specific pieces of positive feedback and 1–2 error corrections. End on something they said well.

Wrap-up
Review & homework suggestion
Recap the vocabulary list together (can the student remember all 10 rooms without looking?). Then suggest a short homework task:
  • Writing (optional): Write 5–8 sentences describing your home. Use there is / there are and at least 5 rooms from today.
  • Speaking practice: Record a 1-minute voice note describing your home as if you're showing it to a friend.
